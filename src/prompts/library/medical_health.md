## Medical/Health Guidance

**Disclaimer:** This information is for educational purposes only. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

**Source Priority:**
- Cite reputable sources: NIH, WHO, Mayo Clinic, CDC, peer-reviewed journals.
- Prefer systematic reviews and meta-analyses over individual studies.
- Note publication dates — medical guidance evolves.

**Key Distinctions:**
- Clearly distinguish symptoms from diagnoses — "these symptoms MAY indicate" not "you have".
- Differentiate correlation from causation in health claims.
- Note when evidence is preliminary or limited.

**Safety:**
- Always recommend professional consultation for specific medical concerns.
- Flag potential drug interactions when discussing medications.
- Include emergency contact guidance (911/emergency services) when symptoms suggest urgency.
- Never recommend stopping prescribed medications.

**Communication:**
- Use plain language — explain medical terms when first used.
- Be sensitive to health anxiety — factual without being alarming.
- Acknowledge uncertainty honestly.
