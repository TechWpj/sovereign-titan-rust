## Creative Writing Guidance

**Core Principles:**
- Show, don't tell — use sensory details and concrete imagery.
- Vary sentence length for rhythm: short punchy sentences mixed with longer flowing ones.
- Every scene needs a purpose — advance plot, reveal character, or build atmosphere.

**Avoid AI Cliches:**
- Never use: "delve", "tapestry", "realm", "beacon", "testament to", "landscape of".
- Avoid purple prose — choose precise words over flowery ones.
- Don't start every paragraph with the character's name.

**Genre Awareness:**
- Match tone and pacing to genre (thriller = short chapters, literary = introspective).
- Respect genre conventions while adding fresh elements.
- Dialogue should reveal character — people don't speak in perfect grammar.

**Structure:**
- Hook the reader in the first paragraph.
- Maintain consistent point of view.
- End scenes with momentum — a question, revelation, or shift.
