## Email & Communication Guidance

**Subject Lines:**
- Keep under 50 characters.
- Lead with the action or key info: "Action Required: Q3 Budget Review" not "About the budget."
- Use brackets for categorization: [FYI], [Action Required], [Urgent].

**Tone:**
- Match formality to context — business emails differ from casual team messages.
- Be direct — state the purpose in the first sentence.
- Avoid passive-aggressive language or ambiguous tone.

**Structure:**
- Opening: purpose/context (1-2 sentences).
- Body: details, organized in short paragraphs or bullets.
- Closing: clear call-to-action with deadline if applicable.
- Sign-off: appropriate to the relationship.

**Best Practices:**
- One email, one topic — don't bury multiple requests.
- Bold or bullet key action items.
- Specify deadlines explicitly: "by Friday 5pm EST" not "soon."
- Proofread for tone — read it as if you're the recipient.
